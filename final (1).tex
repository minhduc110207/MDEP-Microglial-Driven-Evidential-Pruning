\begin{abstract}
Standard Evidential Deep Learning (EDL) quantifies predictive uncertainty through Dirichlet parameterization but retains a fixed network topology, missing the opportunity to leverage uncertainty signals for structural adaptation. Conventional Dynamic Sparse Training (DST) adapts connectivity during training yet relies on magnitude or gradient signals that risk premature removal of critical minority-class features. We propose \textbf{DST-EDL}, a framework coupling EDL-derived vacuity and ambiguity signals with pruning and regrowth under exact 2:4 structured sparsity. A virtual effective-weight probe scores dormant connections through a separate evidential objective without applying supervised parameter updates. On ISIC 2024, a dermatological screening benchmark with below $0.12\%$ malignant prevalence, DST-EDL achieves the highest observed mean $\text{pAUC}_{0.80} = 0.1168$ and $\text{Macro AUROC} = 0.8891$ among compared dense and sparse evidential systems on one fixed patient split averaged over three training seeds. A base-loss controlled comparison shows that the full system's ranking advantage depends jointly on focal modulation and regrowth; the topology controller alone does not outperform simpler sparse baselines under the same objective. These results demonstrate a feasible uncertainty-to-topology interface for structured sparse evidential learning in the studied rare-event screening regime.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

Rare-event screening presents a coupled problem: a model must rank scarce positives in the high-sensitivity region, expose weak or conflicting evidence, and retain a structure that can be mapped to efficient inference. ISIC 2024 makes this tension concrete: malignant cases comprise fewer than $0.12\%$ of the data, and evaluation emphasizes sensitivity above $0.80$ \cite{isic2024slice3d}. We therefore preserve the natural validation, calibration, and test prevalence while applying 1:20 majority subsampling only to the training partition. This protocol favors ranking metrics and validation-selected operating points over default-threshold accuracy.

Existing approaches address these requirements separately. Long-tailed methods alter sampling, losses, margins, logits, representations, or classifier heads \cite{cui2019class,cao2019learning,menon2021long,ren2020balanced,kang2019decoupling,zhong2021improving}. EDL exposes Dirichlet-derived uncertainty signals but normally retains a fixed topology \cite{sensoy2018evidential,malinin2018predictive,charpentier2020posterior,deng2023fisher,chen2023redl,shen2024mirage}. Dynamic sparse training adapts connectivity, but commonly ranks links using magnitude, task gradients, momentum, or optimization-based mask objectives \cite{mocanu2018scalable,dettmers2019sparse,evci2020rigging,lasby2023srigl,ji2024bidst}. The unresolved interface is how to use evidential state as a topology signal while maintaining an exact structured-sparsity constraint.

We propose DST-EDL for this interface. Its prediction loop updates active weights under a cached mask. Its structural loop evaluates an ambiguity-over-vacuity pruning proxy and KL-to-uniform dormant-link influence through a virtual effective-weight probe, updates a bounded latent score, and applies blockwise top-two projection. The scores are local design proxies rather than Bayesian quantities or optimality certificates. The virtual probe is the key technical device: it exposes gradients with respect to zero-valued effective coordinates while keeping dormant parameters outside ordinary task updates.

On one fixed patient-grouped ISIC split, seeds 42, 123, and 456 give the highest mean $\mathrm{pAUC}_{0.80}$ and Macro AUROC among the reported systems. The comparison is descriptive: three model seeds do not measure patient-sampling uncertainty, and objective differences prevent attributing the entire gap to topology alone. Component ablations instead provide within-method evidence that removing regrowth produces the largest degradation. A negative CIFAR-100-LT diagnostic defines the present scope rather than supporting a universal long-tailed claim. Separately, TensorRT measurements on an NVIDIA-layout export verify sparse tactic selection and same-graph batch-throughput acceleration; predictive parity with the ISIC-table checkpoints is not claimed.

\section{Related Work}\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Long-Tailed and Evidential Learning.} Long-tailed learning reweights examples, changes margins or logits, rebalances classifier heads, or modifies representations \cite{cui2019class,cao2019learning,menon2021long,ren2020balanced,kang2019decoupling,zhong2021improving,zhou2020bbn,cui2021parametric}. These methods primarily operate at the data, objective, representation, or output level rather than using uncertainty to update internal sparse connectivity.

EDL maps network outputs to Dirichlet parameters, enabling predictive means and evidence-derived uncertainty proxies in one forward pass \cite{sensoy2018evidential,malinin2018predictive}. Posterior Networks, Fisher EDL, relaxed objectives, and adaptive negative evidence refine how such uncertainty is represented or regularized \cite{charpentier2020posterior,deng2023fisher,chen2023redl,chen2024reedl,yu2024anedl}. Because EDL outputs need not be calibrated Bayesian posterior uncertainty \cite{shen2024mirage}, DST-EDL uses vacuity and expected categorical entropy only as operational structural signals.

TLC combines evidence across experts for long-tailed classification, while AREO couples evidential uncertainty with distributionally robust optimization for open-set detection under imbalance \cite{li2022tlc,sapkota2023areo}. Their uncertainty mechanisms support prediction or optimization; DST-EDL instead uses evidence-derived quantities to rank topology changes.

\paragraph{Probabilistic Pruning and Dynamic Sparse Training.} Bayesian Compression, Variational Dropout, and $L_0$ regularization use probabilistic priors, dropout rates, or stochastic gates for sparsification \cite{louizos2017bayesian,molchanov2017variational,louizos2018learning}. Dirichlet Pruning assigns distributions over channels or neurons for post-processing structured compression \cite{adamczewski2021dirichlet}. These works establish distinct probabilistic pruning mechanisms; they do not use EDL ambiguity and vacuity as online prune--regrow signals.

DST maintains a sparse budget while changing connectivity during training. SET uses pruning with random regrowth, SNFS uses momentum-like regrowth, and RigL combines magnitude pruning with task-gradient regrowth \cite{mocanu2018scalable,dettmers2019sparse,evci2020rigging}. SRigL imposes structured constant fan-in sparsity, BiDST formulates mask search as bilevel optimization, PFFDST reduces update cost through parameter freezing, and PA-DST studies learned permutations for structured sparse layers \cite{lasby2023srigl,ji2024bidst,li2025pffdst,tyagi2025padst}. DST-EDL is distinguished by its EDL-derived online saliency and virtual scoring of dormant coordinates under exact 2:4 projection; it does not claim that these heuristics dominate all alternative saliency rules.

\paragraph{Structured Sparsity and Execution.} NVIDIA 2:4 sparsity requires two active values per eligible group of four and can enable Sparse Tensor Core execution when dtype, shape, layout, compression, and kernel coverage are supported \cite{mishra2021accelerating,nvidiacusparselt,pytorchsemistructured}. Mask validity alone does not imply speedup; backend tactic selection must be measured. This distinction motivates our separate TensorRT diagnostic.

\paragraph{Positioning of DST-EDL.} Existing EDL methods use Dirichlet evidence mainly for prediction-time uncertainty, calibration, open-set detection, or imbalance-aware objectives. Existing probabilistic pruning methods use Bayesian/Dirichlet distributions mainly for post-hoc compression or structured pruning. Existing DST methods update topology mainly from magnitudes, gradients, momentum, bilevel objectives, freezing rules, or learned permutations. DST-EDL differs by using Dirichlet vacuity, expected categorical entropy, and KL-to-uniform evidential influence as online structural saliency signals for pruning and dormant-link regrowth under exact 2:4 sparsity. The virtual effective-weight probe is the distinguishing technical mechanism: it introduces a dense auxiliary tensor initialized at the current masked values and computes structural-objective gradients through all coordinates, including dormant ones. Unlike RigL's task-gradient regrowth, which scores dormant links by $|\partial L_{\text{task}} / \partial w_{ij}|$, the probe uses a separate evidential objective ($L_{\text{grow}}$) whose gradient path is fully decoupled from the supervised weight update. The probe decouples structural scoring from the supervised parameter update at each refresh step; after mask projection, the new topology participates in ordinary task optimization.


\section{Preliminaries: Dirichlet Evidential Signals}\label{sec:math}

For logits $\bm{z}\in\R^K$, DST-EDL maps each class logit to non-negative evidence using Softplus,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:softplus}
    e_c=\ln(1+\exp z_c),\qquad
    \alpha_c=e_c+1,\qquad
    S=\sum_{c=1}^K\alpha_c .
\end{equation}
Softplus is used instead of an output ReLU. ReLU can create zero gradient regions for negative evidence logits. The predictive mean is $\hat p_c=\alpha_c/S$. With uniform Subjective Logic base rate $a_c=1/K$ \cite{josang2016subjective}, this equals
\begin{equation}\label{eq:p_hat}
    \hat p_c=b_c+a_cu_e=\frac{e_c+1}{S},
    \qquad b_c=\frac{e_c}{S},\qquad u_e=\frac{K}{S}.
\end{equation}
Imbalance is handled through the loss, leaving the Dirichlet prior unchanged. The identity $\alpha_c=e_c+1$ and the lower bound $S\ge K$ are also preserved.

Following recent critiques of Evidential Deep Learning~\cite{shen2024mirage}, we treat EDL signals as operational uncertainty proxies, not exact Bayesian posterior quantities. Within this framework, Dirichlet vacuity $u_e=K/S$ serves as a low evidence structural proxy and expected categorical entropy, formulated as:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:u_a}
    u_a = \sum_{c=1}^K \frac{\alpha_c}{S} \left[\psi(S + 1) - \psi(\alpha_c + 1)\right]
\end{equation}
as an ambiguity proxy. In implementation, $u_a$ is clamped below at zero to ensure numerical stability against floating point underflow.

For finite logits, Softplus parameterization guarantees positive Dirichlet parameters ($\alpha_c > 1$), bounded vacuity, and a well-defined predictive mean.  Thus, it prevents probability density singularities at the simplex boundaries during highly imbalanced data regimes. Consequently, these evidential signals provide the mathematical foundation to guide our downstream topology adaptation and uncertainty-aware evaluation protocols. Formal validity statements and proofs are provided in Appendix~\ref{app:proofs}.

\section{DST-EDL Framework}\label{sec:methodology}

For exposition, we refer to the pruning signal as \emph{risk ratio signed pruning}, the regrowth signal as \emph{KL uniform structural regrowth}, and the cached refresh schedule as a \emph{two time scale topology cache}.

DST-EDL separates training into the coupled loops summarized in Fig.~\ref{fig:workflow}. The prediction loop updates active weights at mini-batch frequency under a cached mask $\bm{M}$. Once per post-warmup epoch, the structural loop forms one amortized proxy batch, computes pruning and regrowth saliency, updates latent scores $\bm{V}$, and refreshes $\bm{M}$ by blockwise top-two projection.

The reported model employs topology caching, softened signed first order pruning with pruning strength $0.5$, pruning warmup, KL uniform regrowth, symmetric KL regularization, and evidential focal learning. This conservative configuration favors stable high sensitivity ranking over aggressive sensitivity maximization.

\subsection{2:4 Mask Constrained Sparse Topology}
DST-EDL wraps convolutional kernels and linear projections with binary masks. Prediction weights $\bm{W}^{(l)}$ are optimized by the ordinary training loss. Latent scores $\bm{V}^{(l)}$ determine the mask $\bm{M}^{(l)}$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:wrapped_weights}
    \bm{W}_{\text{eff}}^{(l)}=\bm{W}^{(l)}\odot\bm{M}^{(l)} .
\end{equation}
For each eligible row and complete contiguous 4-block, exactly two entries are active:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:c24}
    \mathcal{C}_{2:4}
    =
    \left\{
    \bm{M}\in\{0,1\}^{d_{\text{out}}\times d_{\text{in}}}:
    \sum_{j=0}^{3} M_{i,4k+j}=2,\ \forall i,k
    \right\}.
\end{equation}
Convolutional tensors are reshaped into rows under the reported performance protocol; the deployment profile instead forms KCRS groups along the backend-required input-channel axis. In either profile, projection is independent within each complete eligible 4-block:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mask}
    \begin{aligned}
    I_{i,k}^{(l)} &= \TopTwo_{j\in\{0,1,2,3\}}
    V_{\text{flat},i,4k+j}^{(l)},\\
    M_{\text{flat},i,4k+j}^{(l)} &=
    \begin{cases}
        1 & \text{if } j\in I_{i,k}^{(l)},\\
        0 & \text{otherwise.}
    \end{cases}
    \end{aligned}
\end{equation}
This is equivalent to $\bm{M}^{(t+1)}=\Pi_{\mathcal{C}_{2:4}}(\bm{V}^{(t)})$. The projection maximizes $\langle \bm{M},\bm{V}\rangle$ independently in each complete block. Coordinates outside a complete 4-block remain dense, are excluded from the valid-block denominator, and are outside the exact 2:4 claim. The RGB stem and classifier head are dense by design; channel ordering is the frozen identity map. Appendix~\ref{app:24_feasibility} states the corresponding feasibility boundary.

\subsection{Uncertainty Guided Pruning and KL uniform Structural Regrowth}
At each structural refresh, DST-EDL evaluates a proxy batch and computes two saliency signals. The pruning signal uses the ambiguity over vacuity ratio. The regrowth signal uses KL to uniform evidential influence. Ordinary prediction uses $\bm{W}\odot\bm{M}$. Structural scoring uses an auxiliary dense effective weight probe $\widetilde{\bm{W}}_{\text{eff}}$ initialized at the current masked values:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:virtual_probe}
\begin{aligned}
    \widetilde{\bm{W}}_{\text{eff}}
    &\leftarrow \bm{W}\odot\bm{M},\\
    \nabla_{\widetilde{\bm{W}}_{\text{eff}}}(\cdot)
    &\text{ is used only for mask scoring.}
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
This probe gives dormant coordinates structural saliency without applying ordinary parameter updates to dormant weights. The formal distinction from masked training gradients is included in Appendix~\ref{app:virtual_probe_note}.

\paragraph{Proxy construction.} At each structural refresh, the implementation concatenates the first four shuffled training mini-batches (up to 128 images at batch size 32) and uses that same tensor for pruning, regrowth, and anti-crystallization signals. Sampling follows the natural 1:20 training frequency; it is neither class-balanced nor guaranteed to contain a malignant example. A label check reports when fewer than two classes occur, but does not resample or alter the proxy. A new loader iteration is used at each epoch under the fixed split and model seed. The probe runs with BatchNorm statistics frozen and is discarded after scoring.

For pruning, the proxy batch risk ratio is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:batch_risk}
    R_{\mathcal{B}}
    =
    \frac{1}{|\mathcal{B}|}
    \sum_{n\in\mathcal{B}}
    \frac{u_a^{(n)}}{u_e^{(n)}+\epsilon}.
\end{equation}
We formulate this ratio as a design proxy with three explicit properties: monotonic increase with ambiguity, attenuation of pruning pressure under extreme vacuity, and boundedness on finite proxy batches. Establishing superiority over entropy, margin, Fisher-style, or task-gradient signals remains outside the present evidence. We note that $R_{\mathcal{B}}$ decreases both when ambiguity drops (desirable) and when vacuity increases (evidence loss), so the proxy does not distinguish the two mechanisms; the positive-part operator and subsequent task training provide implicit but not guaranteed disambiguation. For active links, signed first-order pruning is defined as:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:C_ij}
    C_{ij}
    =
    \operatorname{Rank}_{l}\!\left(
    \pospart{w_{\text{eff},ij}
    \frac{\partial R_{\mathcal B}}{\partial w_{\text{eff},ij}}}
    \right),
    \qquad
    \pospart{x}=\max(x,0).
\end{equation}
Here $\operatorname{Rank}_{l}$ is computed over all strictly positive raw saliency values in a layer. Exact zeros receive rank zero; equal values receive their average normalized rank; the single-positive case receives rank one. The positive part keeps only removals locally predicted to reduce the proxy ratio. Gradients are detached before the latent-score update. Appendix~\ref{app:signed_pruning} gives the local Taylor interpretation; it is not a descent or optimality guarantee.

For regrowth, the reported setting uses KL uniform regrowth:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:grow_objective}
    L_{\text{grow}}
    =
    \frac{1}{|\mathcal{B}|}\sum_{n\in\mathcal{B}}
    \KL\!\left(
    \Dir(\bm{\alpha}^{(n)})\middle\|\Dir(\mathbf{1})
    \right).
\end{equation}
Its absolute probe gradient rank defines dormant link influence:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:G_ij}
    G_{ij}
    =
    \operatorname{Rank}_{l}\!\left(
    \left|
    \frac{\partial L_{\text{grow}}}
    {\partial w_{\text{eff},ij}}
    \right|
    \right).
\end{equation}
This quantity ranks coordinates by local influence on the KL objective relative to the uninformative Dirichlet reference. Its absolute value does not identify the direction of evidence change, the affected class, or a guaranteed discrimination benefit. The supervised objective and subsequent block projection determine the realized effect.

The latent score update separates active retention from dormant reactivation. First, we compute the raw structural step $\Delta V_{ij}^{(t)}$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:score_step}
\begin{aligned}
    A_{ij}^{(t)} &= G_{ij}^{(t)}-\lambda_p C_{ij}^{(t)},\\
    D_{ij}^{(t)} &= \rho G_{ij}^{(t)}+\xi_{ij}^{(t)}\cdot\mathbb{1}\!\left[G_{ij}^{(t)}\le\tau\right],\\
    \Delta V_{ij}^{(t)} &= M_{ij}^{(t)}A_{ij}^{(t)} +\left(1-M_{ij}^{(t)}\right)D_{ij}^{(t)}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
Here, active links ($M_{ij}=1$) are penalized by the signed removal pressure $C_{ij}$, while dormant links ($M_{ij}=0$) are guided by the attenuated regrowth score $\rho G_{ij}$. Exploration noise $\xi_{ij}$ (a tie-breaking stability heuristic that does not improve mean pAUC in the reported configuration; see Appendix~\ref{app:ablation}) is not applied unconditionally: it fires only when $G_{ij}^{(t)}\le\tau$ ($\tau=10^{-8}$ in the reported configuration), i.e., when the regrowth saliency is numerically flat, typically the result of tied raw gradients collapsing to the same rank. Active-link scores are already separated from dormant candidates by the $-\lambda_p C_{ij}$ term and do not require this tie-breaking mechanism. To ensure topological stability, this step is smoothed via an exponential moving average, mean-centered to prevent global drift, and clamped to a bounded morphological state:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:score_update}
\begin{aligned}
    \mu_{ij}^{(t)} &= \beta_m\mu_{ij}^{(t - 1)} +(1-\beta_m)\Delta V_{ij}^{(t)},\\
    X_{ij}^{(t)} &= V_{ij}^{(t - 1)}+\eta_{\text{struct}}\mu_{ij}^{(t)},\\
    V_{ij}^{(t)} &= \operatorname{clamp}\!\left( X_{ij}^{(t)}-\operatorname{mean}(\bm{X}^{(t)}), -V_{\max}, V_{\max}\right).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The clamp in Eq.~\ref{eq:score_update} bounds the latent morphology state, and Eq.~\ref{eq:mask} restores exact valid block 2:4 feasibility after each refresh.
Pruning changes only the mask: the underlying dormant value in $\bm W$ is retained. Outside warmup, its task gradient, optimizer moments, and weight decay are masked. If top-two projection reactivates that coordinate, training resumes from the stored value; there is no zero or random reinitialization. Residual equal-score ties follow the backend top-$k$ selection order; no additional random tie-break is used except for the explicitly gated anti-crystallization noise above.
For active links, $G_{ij}^{(t)}$ acts as a retention and influence score.
$C_{ij}^{(t)}$ supplies signed removal pressure. For dormant links, the
attenuated regrowth score $\rho G_{ij}^{(t)}$ and optional tie breaking noise
$\xi_{ij}^{(t)}$ control reactivation. The final clamp bounds the latent
morphology state, and Eq.~\ref{eq:mask} restores exact valid block 2:4
feasibility after each refresh.

\subsection{Gradient Isolation, Caching, and Stability Diagnostics}
Ordinary training uses a fixed cached mask for each mini batch. Prediction gradients and weight decay update only active links. For any differentiable mini batch loss evaluated through $\bm{W}_{\text{eff}}=\bm{W}\odot\bm{M}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:masked_gradient}
    \nabla_{\bm{W}} L
    =
    \nabla_{\bm{W}_{\text{eff}}} L \odot \bm{M}.
\end{equation}
Dormant parameters receive zero ordinary task gradient under a fixed mask. DST-EDL separates this training path from the virtual structural probe in Eq.~\ref{eq:virtual_probe} (Sec.~\ref{sec:methodology}).

\paragraph{Structural cost and validity boundary.} Each post-warmup refresh adds one proxy forward pass, two scalar backward passes for pruning and regrowth, and one activation-gradient extraction; it is amortized over the subsequent epoch. We do not report a wall-clock training-overhead claim. The derivations establish that the signals are finite under the stated parameterization and that projection preserves eligible-block feasibility. They do not establish convergence, topology optimality, Bayesian correctness, predictive improvement, or deployment speed. Appendix~\ref{app:proofs} makes these boundaries explicit.

\section{Optimization Framework}\label{sec:optimization}

To address extreme imbalance, we define an evidential focal objective (EFL) by combining the expected Dirichlet classification loss of EDL \cite{sensoy2018evidential} with focal modulation \cite{lin2017focal}. This is our implemented objective, not a claim of reproducing a separately published method named ``Evidential Focal Loss.'' The reported configuration uses square-root class-frequency dampening, stop-gradient focal modulation, KL annealing, and symmetric KL regularization.

In the reported symmetric setting, the KL multiplier is exactly uniform across samples and classes. The asymmetric KL multiplier is retained only for the diagnostic ablation named ``asymmetric KL''. The reported DST-EDL row uses the symmetric objective:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:efl}
\begin{aligned}
    L_{\text{EFL}}
    &= \frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=1}^N \mathcal{F}_n(t)
    \Bigl[
    \bar{\omega}_{y_n}L_{\text{CE}}^{(n)} \\
    &\hspace{2.4em}
    + \lambda_{\text{KL}}\mu(t)
    L_{\text{KL}}^{(n)}
    \Bigr].
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The dampened class weights are normalized so that the majority class has weight one and then capped:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sample_weight}
\begin{aligned}
    \omega_c
    &= \sqrt{\frac{N_{\text{majority}}}{N_c}},
    &\bar{\omega}_c
    &= \min(\omega_c,\omega_{\max}),\\
    \omega_{\text{majority}}
    &= 1,
    &\omega_{\max}
    &= 10.0 .
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The square root form damps inverse frequency scaling relative to direct inverse prevalence weighting. The cap bounds the imbalance modulation. The expected cross entropy under the Dirichlet predictive distribution is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:dirichlet_ce}
    L_{\text{CE}}^{(n)}
    = \sum_{c=1}^K y_c^{(n)}
    \left[\psi(S^{(n)})-\psi(\alpha_c^{(n)})\right].
\end{equation}
The focal term is applied with stop gradient confidence so that focal weighting changes sample emphasis without directly distorting the Dirichlet evidence:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:focal_schedule}
\begin{aligned}
    \mathcal{F}_n(t)
    &= \left(1-\operatorname{sg}\!\left[
    \hat{p}_{y_n}^{(n)}\right]\right)^{\gamma(t)},\\
    \gamma(t)
    &= \begin{cases}
    0, & t < T_w, \\
    \frac{\gamma_{\max}}{2}
    \left(1+\cos\left(\pi
    \frac{t - T_w}{T - T_w}\right)\right), & t \ge T_w,
    \end{cases}\\
    \gamma_{\max} &= 5.0 .
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
This is the effective EFL exponent.
For KL regularization, we remove the true class evidence from the penalty by defining
$\tilde{\alpha}_c^{(n)}=y_c^{(n)}+(1-y_c^{(n)})\alpha_c^{(n)}$. The adjusted KL term is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:kl_loss_main}
    L_{\text{KL}}^{(n)}
    =
    \KL\!\left(\Dir(\tilde{\bm{\alpha}}^{(n)})\parallel\Dir(\mathbf{1})\right).
\end{equation}
The annealing coefficient is $\mu(t)=\min(1.0,t/t_{\text{anneal}})$ with $t_{\text{anneal}}=10$. In the reported symmetric setting, the KL regularizer treats all classes uniformly, equivalent to multiplying $L_{\text{KL}}^{(n)}$ by $1.0$. The asymmetric KL diagnostic variant instead uses a capped class dependent multiplier (formula detailed in Appendix~\ref{app:proofs}). The reported model applies $\bar{\omega}_{y_n}$ to the supervised CE term.
The resulting training objective is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:total_loss}
    L_{\text{total}}=L_{\text{EFL}}(\bm{e},\bm{y},t).
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Controlled imbalance modulation.}
For each sample $n$, the stop gradient focal factor $\mathcal{F}_n(t)$ reweights the Dirichlet CE/KL gradients with respect to the evidence parameters. It blocks an additional derivative path through $\hat{p}_{y_n}^{(n)}$. The supervised class weight is bounded by $\bar{\omega}_{y_n}\le\omega_{\max}$. Focal modulation therefore changes sample weights without adding a confidence feedback loop through $\hat{p}_{y_n}$.

\paragraph{Training Warmup.}
We use a short learning rate and loss scale warmup for numerical stability. Its schedule is an implementation detail and is given with the full training algorithm in Appendix~\ref{app:algorithm}.


\paragraph{Post hoc Calibration.}
Post hoc calibration procedures are detailed in Appendix~\ref{app:algorithm}.

\paragraph{Threshold selection rule.}
Let $\hat{y}_n(\tau) = \mathbb{1}[\hat{p}_{\text{mal},n} \ge \tau]$ denote the binary prediction at threshold $\tau$. The reported high-sensitivity operating point selects
\begin{equation}\label{eq:threshold}
    \tau^* = \argmax_{\tau \in \mathcal{T}} \;
    \text{Specificity}_{\text{val}}(\tau)
    \quad \text{s.t.} \quad
    \text{Sensitivity}_{\text{val}}(\tau) \ge 0.80,
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{T}$ is a grid of $10{,}001$ evenly spaced values in $[0,1]$ and metrics are computed on the validation partition only. The selected $\tau^*$ is frozen before test evaluation. All reported models use this identical selection rule.

\section{Experimental Setup}\label{sec:experiment}

\subsection{Evaluation Benchmark}
The main empirical claims are centered on ISIC 2024. Appendix~\ref{app:external} reports a completed CIFAR-100-LT 1:100 diagnostic. Additional imbalance profiles, backbones, and realized sparse kernel profiling remain future validation targets unless explicitly reported. The current evidence supports feasibility and stability under the reported case study, with no domain general SOTA claim.

\paragraph{ISIC 2024 Case Study.} We instantiate DST-EDL on the ISIC 2024 Challenge dataset~\cite{isic2024slice3d}. It contains 3D-TBP images with fewer than $0.12\%$ minority-class prevalence. To prevent data leakage, we use a stratified 70/5/5/20 train/validation/calibration/test split grouped by patient\_id with split seed 42. The resulting partitions contain 5,775 training images, 21,930 validation images, 24,235 calibration images, and 65,931 held out test images. The test partition contains 77 malignant and 65,854 benign samples ($0.117\%$ test prevalence). The 5,775 training count is post-subsampling; the approximate 70/5/5/20 ratios refer to the pre-subsampled dataset, with patient grouping causing slight deviations from exact proportions. The validation partition is used for decision threshold sweeps. The calibration partition is used for temperature and bias calibration. The held out test partition is used for final reporting. Images are resized to $224\times224$ and normalized using ImageNet-1K statistics.

\paragraph{Metrics.} Default thresholds are unreliable under extreme prevalence skew. We report validation-selected high-sensitivity operating points (Eq.~\ref{eq:threshold}) together with threshold independent ranking and calibration metrics. The main comparison reports Balanced Accuracy, pAUC$_{0.80}$, Macro AUROC, PR AUC, adaptive ECE, and valid block 2:4 feasibility. AURC is reported in the seed level and component diagnostic tables. Sensitivity and Specificity are reported when available for operating point diagnostics. Lower AURC indicates better risk ordering. Lower ECE indicates better calibration. AURC is computed from the empirical risk coverage curve using the same unnormalized scale across methods. pAUC$_{0.80}$ is the unnormalized partial area under the ROC curve in the TPR interval $[0.80, 1.0]$ (maximum value $0.20$), computed with trapezoidal interpolation and arithmetically averaged across seeds.

\subsection{Baselines and Backbones}
\paragraph{Baselines and comparison contract.} The main table includes Dense, Fisher, Flexible, and relaxed EDL; Static 2:4 EDL; RigL-style 2:4; and DST-EDL. All rows share the patient split, training subsample, ResNet-18 architecture and ImageNet-initialization policy, epoch budget, learning-rate schedule, final-epoch checkpoint rule, calibration partition, bias--temperature calibration family, and validation-only threshold selection. They do \emph{not} share an identical objective: Dense/Static/RigL-style EDL use their base symmetric evidential loss without focal modulation, whereas DST-EDL adds the objective in Eq.~\ref{eq:efl}. Consequently, Table~\ref{tab:main_evidential_comparison} is an end-to-end system comparison, not a controlled estimate of the topology signal's isolated causal effect. Appendix~\ref{app:ablation} instead changes one DST-EDL component at a time, including EFL removal. Softmax long-tailed baselines and CIFAR-100-LT are reported as separate scope diagnostics.

\paragraph{Backbone Instantiation and Wrapping.}
The reported case study uses a ResNet-18 instantiation. Inside each residual block, the $3 \times 3$ convolutional layers are replaced with sparse convolutional wrappers. Their masks are generated by latent scores. The first input convolution is kept dense. The structural proxy pass computes the risk ratio gradient for the pruner. It also computes KL to uniform gradient saliency for the regrower. Appendix~\ref{app:seed_level} documents additional sparse diagnostics as implementation evidence, not separate contributions.

\paragraph{Implementation Details and Profile Separation.} Models use AMP~\cite{micikevicius2018mixed}, with evidential losses evaluated in FP32. The fixed performance-evaluation protocol uses AdamW, cosine annealing over 40 epochs, one epoch of learning-rate warmup, final-epoch checkpoints, train-only 1:20 subsampling, and seeds 42/123/456 with split seed 42. Square-root weights are computed from the subsampled training partition. The natural class prior used for prior correction is estimated from the unsampled training patients before subsampling, never from calibration or test data. Bias and temperature are fit only on the calibration partition; thresholds are selected only on validation. Adaptive ECE uses 15 equal-frequency bins. The separate NVIDIA-layout profile forms convolutional groups along the KCRS input-channel axis for TensorRT; its predictive metrics are not substituted into the ISIC tables.

\subsection{Sparse 2:4 Baseline Comparison}
DST-EDL outperforms Static 2:4 EDL in balanced accuracy ($0.8110$ vs.~$0.7956$), pAUC$_{0.80}$ ($0.1168$ vs.~$0.0965$), and Macro AUROC ($0.8891$ vs.~$0.8663$). Versus RigL-style 2:4, the means are $0.1168$ vs.~$0.1096$, $0.8891$ vs.~$0.8829$, and $0.8110$ vs.~$0.7696$. Static 2:4 remains stronger in PR AUC and ECE. Lacking focal modulation in these baselines, the table supports a system tradeoff, not isolated topology attribution.

\paragraph{Sparse morphology diagnostics.}
All sparse models preserved 50.0\% active density with no structural collapse (Appendix~\ref{app:seed_level}). All wrapped layers in DST-EDL satisfied 2:4 block constraints (2,789,376/2,789,376 blocks). Feasibility and TensorRT execution are evaluated separately.

\subsection{Seed Level Stability and Operating Point Tradeoffs}
Across seeds, DST-EDL obtains mean balanced accuracy $0.8110$, sensitivity $0.7835$, specificity $0.8386$, pAUC$_{0.80}=0.1168$, and Macro AUROC $0.8891$ (Appendix~\ref{app:seed_level}). DST-EDL displays lower training-seed SD than RigL-style 2:4 (e.g., $0.0050$ vs.~$0.0608$ in balanced accuracy), though three seeds cannot establish statistical variance ordering.

\paragraph{Statistical qualification.}
Three seeds on one split measure seed variation, not cohort-sampling uncertainty. A paired test-set bootstrap could quantify sampling uncertainty conditional on this split; split-selection uncertainty requires retraining and exceeds the budget. We report seed SD as a lower bound on variation and omit $p$-value claims. The pAUC$_{0.80}$ gap between DST-EDL and RigL ($0.0072$) is approximately one seed SD, making it descriptive rather than statistically established.

\subsection{Controlled Comparison: Topology Under Matched and Focal Objectives}
Three findings emerge: (1) Under the base loss, DST-EDL w/o EFL ($0.0898$) matches Dense EDL ($0.0885$) in pAUC but trails Static ($0.0965$) and RigL ($0.1096$), indicating the topology controller alone does not improve ranking. (2) Ranking gains rely on focal modulation; adding EFL lifts pAUC to $0.1168$ and Macro AUROC to $0.8891$. (3) Whether this reflects a topology$\times$EFL interaction or is EFL-dominated is unquantified due to the lack of EFL-matched sparse baselines.

\subsection{Component Attribution}
We analyze component ablations on the fixed ISIC split (Table~\ref{tab:controlled}; Appendix~\ref{app:ablation}):
\paragraph{Regrowth dominance.} Removing KL-uniform regrowth causes the largest degradation: pAUC$_{0.80}$ drops from $0.1168$ to $0.0640$ ($-45\%$) and Macro AUROC to $0.7663$. This indicates that dormant-link reactivation is the most critical structural mechanism.
\paragraph{Pruner contribution.} Removing the signed ambiguity-over-vacuity pruner yields a smaller decrease (pAUC$_{0.80}$ to $0.1051$, Macro AUROC to $0.8720$), suggesting regrowth scheduling contributes more than pruning in this regime.
\paragraph{Focal modulation dependence.} Removing focal modulation (``w/o EFL'') preserves topology search but drops sensitivity from $0.7835$ to $0.5628$. Without EFL, the topology controller underperforms RigL-style 2:4 in pAUC$_{0.80}$ ($0.0898$ vs.~$0.1096$), confirming EFL is a necessary co-factor.

\section{Limitations and Broader Impact}\label{sec:limitations}
We qualify our findings with the following scope boundaries:
\begin{itemize}
    \setlength{\itemsep}{0pt}
    \setlength{\parskip}{0pt}
    \setlength{\parsep}{0pt}
    \item \textbf{Single split.} ISIC claims rest on one patient split with 77 malignant test cases. Three seeds measure training-seed variation; cohort-sampling uncertainty remains unquantified.
    \item \textbf{Metric tradeoff.} DST-EDL achieves the highest observed mean pAUC$_{0.80}$ and Macro AUROC, but Dense EDL is stronger in balanced accuracy/specificity, and Static 2:4 has higher PR AUC.
    \item \textbf{Objective confounding.} Table~\ref{tab:controlled} lacks EFL-matched sparse baselines (e.g., RigL+EFL). The topology$\times$EFL interaction therefore remains unquantified.
    \item \textbf{Regrowth alternatives.} Ablations show regrowth necessity but do not compare KL-to-uniform regrowth against all task-gradient, entropy, or class-conditioned alternatives.
    \item \textbf{CIFAR negative.} DST-EDL trails standard CE on CIFAR-100-LT; domain-general long-tailed superiority is not claimed.
    \item \textbf{Deployment scope.} The $1.14\times$ TensorRT speedup applies only to one device (RTX A2000) at batch 64. Cross-hardware portability and batch-1 latency remain untested.
\end{itemize}
These boundaries preserve the central contribution: a well-defined uncertainty-to-topology interface, a controlled comparison, and an executable sparse path.

\paragraph{Broader Impact.} As dermatological screening carries clinical false-negative risks, DST-EDL is a research triage tool, not a diagnostic system. Subgroup fairness across skin tones, demographics, and acquisition sites is unexamined, and external domain shift is untested. Deployments require human expert review for deferred cases and strict patient privacy controls.

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
We introduced DST-EDL, an uncertainty-to-topology framework combining local EDL saliency, a virtual dormant-link probe, and exact 2:4 projection. Under the three-seed ISIC protocol, it achieves the highest observed mean pAUC$_{0.80}$ ($0.1168$) and Macro AUROC ($0.8891$), with tradeoffs in calibration and specificity. Separate TensorRT experiments verify a 1.14$\times$ same-graph batch throughput gain, establishing sparse-path executability. Thus, evidential state can guide dynamic structured sparsity in rare-event screening, while broader generalization remains an empirical question.

\section{Reproducibility Details}
\label{app:reproducibility}

\subsection*{1. Experimental Setting and Hyperparameters}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Random Seeds:} Main ISIC comparisons use model seeds 42, 123, and 456 with split seed 42. Paper level component ablations include only variants that completed all three model seeds. Partial single seed or two seed ablations are excluded from the reported ablation table.
    \item \textbf{Preprocessing \& Dimensions:} Images are resized to $224 \times 224$ and normalized using ImageNet channel wise statistics: $\mu = [0.485, 0.456, 0.406]$ and $\sigma = [0.229, 0.224, 0.225]$. Data augmentation includes horizontal and vertical flips.
    \item \textbf{Optimizer and Learning Rate Schedule:} We employ the AdamW optimizer ($\beta_1=0.9$, $\beta_2=0.999$, weight decay $10^{-4}$ on active weights). The learning rate is initialized at $1\times10^{-6}$ and linearly warmed up to $4\times10^{-5}$ over the first epoch, followed by a cosine annealing schedule \cite{loshchilov2016sgdr} over 40 epochs.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{2. Evaluation Protocols}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Validation/Test Split Rules:} The ISIC 2024 dataset is partitioned into 70\% training, 5\% validation, 5\% calibration, and 20\% test sets using split seed 42. The resulting split contains 5,775 training images, 21,930 validation images, 24,235 calibration images, and 65,931 test images, with 77 malignant and 65,854 benign test cases. Patient-grouped stratification prevents a patient from appearing in more than one partition. The test set is excluded from training, calibration, model selection, and threshold selection and is used only for final reporting after each configuration is fixed.
    \item \textbf{Threshold Selection Protocol:} For ISIC, operating points are selected by performing threshold sweeps on the isolated validation partition to hit target clinical sensitivities ($\ge 80\%$). Temperature and bias parameters are fitted on the separate calibration partition. Validation derived thresholds are frozen before final test evaluation.
    \item \textbf{ECE Binning and pAUC:} The reported ECE is adaptive ECE with 15 equal frequency bins. Equal width ECE is also computed as an auxiliary diagnostic. pAUC$_{0.80}$ is the unnormalized partial area under the ROC curve within $\text{TPR} \in [0.80, 1.0]$ (maximum value $0.20$), computed with trapezoidal interpolation and arithmetically averaged across seeds.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{3. Source Code and Data Releases}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Submission Artifact:} The anonymized supplementary archive accompanying submission contains the sparse wrapper layers, loss definitions, experiment runners, environment requirements, fixed seeds, and evaluation scripts needed to reconstruct the reported protocol. The artifact manifest records the source revision; no post-publication release promise is used as evidence of reproducibility.
\end{itemize}

\section{DST-EDL Algorithm and Implementation Details}
\label{app:algorithm}
This appendix gives the procedural details that are referenced from the
optimization section. It contains the warmup rule, the post hoc evidential
calibration formula, the full training loop, and the implementation
configuration used for the reported ISIC 2024 case study.

\paragraph{Learning rate and loss scale warmup.}
During the first epoch, the learning rate is linearly warmed from $\eta_{\text{init}}=10^{-6}$ to $\eta_{\text{base}}=4\times10^{-5}$. An initial loss multiplier decays from $S_{\max}=4.0$ to $1.0$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:warmup_main}
\begin{aligned}
    \eta_s
    &=
    \eta_{\text{init}}
    +(\eta_{\text{base}}-\eta_{\text{init}})
    \frac{s}{S_{\text{warmup}}},\\
    S_{\text{loss}}(s)
    &=
    S_{\max}-(S_{\max}-1)
    \frac{s}{S_{\text{warmup}}}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The scaled objective $L_{\text{scaled}}=S_{\text{loss}}(s)L_{\text{total}}$ is used during warmup and $L_{\text{total}}$ afterward.

\paragraph{Bias corrected evidential calibration.}
After training, logits are corrected before the Softplus evidence map by
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
    z^{\mathrm{cal}}_c
    &=
    \frac{z^{\mathrm{base}}_c+\Delta^\pi_c}{T_{\mathrm{cal}}}
    + b_{\mathrm{res},c},\\
    \Delta^\pi_c
    &=
    \log(\pi_{\mathrm{true},c}+\epsilon)
    -\log(\pi_{\mathrm{train},c}+\epsilon).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
Temperature only calibration sets $\bm{b}_{\mathrm{res}}=\bm{0}$. The reported bias temperature mode learns both $T_{\mathrm{cal}}$ and $\bm{b}_{\mathrm{res}}$ on the calibration partition.

AUROC from $0.8891$ to $0.7663$, PR AUC from $0.0355$ to $0.0077$, and AURC
from $0.00023$ to $0.00057$. This supports the need for dormant-link
reactivation in this controller, but the ablation does not compare KL regrowth
against every alternative regrowth rule. Removing the pruner yields smaller
decreases in balanced accuracy, sensitivity, pAUC$_{0.80}$, and Macro AUROC.
Asymmetric KL remains close in ranking metrics but moves the operating point
toward higher specificity and lower sensitivity. Removing focal modulation
improves ECE and specificity while sensitivity falls from $0.7835$ to $0.5628$.
Removing anti-crystallization gives a slightly higher mean pAUC$_{0.80}$
($0.1173$ vs. $0.1168$) and a larger sensitivity SD. These are descriptive
component effects on one split; no significance claim is made.

\subsection{Compact Dirichlet Identities}
For completeness, the Dirichlet density for $\bm{p}$ on the probability simplex is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:dirichlet_pdf_app}
\begin{aligned}
    \Dir(\bm{p}\mid\bm{\alpha})
    &=
    \frac{1}{B(\bm{\alpha})}
    \prod_{c=1}^{K}p_c^{\alpha_c - 1}\\
    &=
    \frac{\Gamma(S)}{\prod_{c=1}^{K}\Gamma(\alpha_c)}
    \prod_{c=1}^{K}p_c^{\alpha_c - 1}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The implementation uses the following identities from the main text:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
    \hat{p}_c&=\frac{\alpha_c}{S},
    &
    u_e&=\frac{K}{S},\\
    u_a&=\sum_{c=1}^{K}\frac{\alpha_c}{S}
    \left[\psi(S+1)-\psi(\alpha_c+1)\right].
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
Under the Softplus evidence parameterization, $\alpha_c>1$ for finite logits. Therefore $S>K$. Hence $u_e\in(0,1)$. Its supremum $1$ is approached as all evidence tends to zero. Also, $S\ge \alpha_c$ and $\psi(\cdot)$ is monotone on positive inputs. Each entropy summand is non negative. Hence $u_a\ge0$.

\subsection{Structural Risk Ratio Chain Rule}
\label{app:r_chain}
The batch structural risk ratio $R_{\mathcal B}$ in Eq.~\ref{eq:batch_risk} is differentiable for $\epsilon>0$ under the Softplus evidence map when the current mask is held fixed during the proxy structural pass. Write $\partial_{ij}$ for the derivative with respect to $w_{\text{eff},ij}$. The weight derivative is the batch average of the per sample ratio derivatives:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
    \partial_{ij} R_{\mathcal B}
    &=
    \frac{1}{|\mathcal B|}\sum_{n\in\mathcal B}
    \left[
    \frac{1}{u_e+\epsilon}\partial_{ij}u_a
    -
    \frac{u_a}{(u_e+\epsilon)^2}
    \partial_{ij}u_e
    \right]^{(n)} .
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
At the scalar level, $r(u_a,u_e)=u_a/(u_e+\epsilon)$ satisfies
\[
    \frac{\partial r}{\partial u_a}
    =
    \frac{1}{u_e+\epsilon}>0,
    \qquad
    \frac{\partial r}{\partial u_e}
    =
    -\frac{u_a}{(u_e+\epsilon)^2}\le 0,
\]
The proxy is monotone increasing in ambiguity and monotone decreasing in vacuity whenever $u_a\ge0$.
The vacuity derivative expands through the Dirichlet parameters as
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\partial u_e}{\partial w_{\text{eff},ij}}
    =
    \sum_{m=1}^{K}
    \frac{\partial u_e}{\partial \alpha_m}
    \frac{\partial \alpha_m}{\partial z_m}
    \frac{\partial z_m}{\partial w_{\text{eff},ij}},
    \qquad
    \frac{\partial u_e}{\partial \alpha_m}
    =
    -\frac{K}{S^2},
\end{equation}
where $\frac{\partial \alpha_m}{\partial z_m}=1/(1+\exp(-z_m))$ for $\alpha_m=\ln(1+\exp(z_m))+1$. For the entropy proxy, let $\psi_1(\cdot)$ denote the trigamma function and $\delta_{cm}$ the Kronecker delta. Then
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
    \frac{\partial u_a}{\partial \alpha_m}
    ={}&
    \sum_{c=1}^{K}
    \frac{\delta_{cm}S-\alpha_c}{S^2}
    \left[\psi(S+1)-\psi(\alpha_c+1)\right]\\
    &+
    \sum_{c=1}^{K}
    \frac{\alpha_c}{S}
    \left[
    \psi_1(S+1)-\delta_{cm}\psi_1(\alpha_c+1)
    \right],
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
This gives
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\partial u_a}{\partial w_{\text{eff},ij}}
    =
    \sum_{m=1}^{K}
    \frac{\partial u_a}{\partial \alpha_m}
    \frac{\partial \alpha_m}{\partial z_m}
    \frac{\partial z_m}{\partial w_{\text{eff},ij}} .
\end{equation}
This expansion provides the batch risk ratio derivative used by the signed pruning score in Eq.~\ref{eq:C_ij}. Throughout the structural proxy derivations, derivatives are taken with respect to the effective coordinate $w_{\text{eff},ij}$. When the mask is fixed and the coordinate is active, this corresponds to the ordinary weight derivative up to the fixed mask multiplier. The expression combines local vacuity and expected entropy derivatives through the ordinary network Jacobian $\partial z_m/\partial w_{\text{eff},ij}$, giving an explicit pruning signal.

\subsection{Proxy Batch Concentration of the Risk Ratio Signal}
\label{app:proxy_concentration}
Fix the current weights and mask during a structural refresh, and let
$r(x)=u_a(x)/(u_e(x)+\epsilon)$ under the proxy sampling distribution.
For a $K$-class categorical variable, entropy is bounded above by
$\log K$, hence the Dirichlet expected categorical entropy satisfies
$0\le u_a(x)\le\log K$. Since $u_e(x)+\epsilon\ge\epsilon$, we have
\[
    0\le r(x)\le \frac{\log K}{\epsilon}.
\]
Let $\bar R=\mathbb{E}[r(x)]$ and
$R_{\mathcal B}=|\mathcal B|^{-1}\sum_{x\in\mathcal B}r(x)$ for an
i.i.d. proxy batch from this sampling distribution. Hoeffding's
inequality gives, for any $\delta\in(0,1)$, with probability at least
$1-\delta$,
\[
    |R_{\mathcal B}-\bar R|
    \le
    \frac{\log K}{\epsilon}
    \sqrt{\frac{\log(2/\delta)}{2|\mathcal B|}}.
\]
This result controls the estimation noise of the scalar proxy under the chosen
proxy sampling distribution. Validation risk improvement, convergence, and
dominance over magnitude or task gradient based sparse training remain outside
the statement.

\subsection{KL Regularization Note}
The reported DST-EDL configuration uses the symmetric KL penalty in Eq.~\ref{eq:kl_loss_main}: the KL multiplier is $1.0$ for every sample and class. The adjusted false evidence parameter $\tilde{\alpha}_c^{(n)}=y_c^{(n)}+(1-y_c^{(n)})\alpha_c^{(n)}$ defines the compact KL term. Its expanded form is the standard Dirichlet to Dirichlet KL with the true class concentration removed from the shrinkage penalty:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:kl_loss_expanded}
\begin{aligned}
    L_{\text{KL}}^{(n)}
    ={}& \ln \left(
    \frac{\Gamma\left(\sum_{c=1}^K\tilde{\alpha}_c^{(n)}\right)}
    {\Gamma(K)\prod_{c=1}^K\Gamma(\tilde{\alpha}_c^{(n)})}
    \right)\\
    &+\sum_{c=1}^K(\tilde{\alpha}_c^{(n)}-1)
    \left[
    \psi(\tilde{\alpha}_c^{(n)})
    -\psi\!\left(\sum_{c=1}^K\tilde{\alpha}_c^{(n)}\right)
    \right].
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Validity of Evidential State}
Softplus is strictly positive for finite real valued logits. Hence $\alpha_c=e_c+1>1$ and $S=\sum_c\alpha_c>K$. The expected probabilities obey $\hat{p}_c=\alpha_c/S>0$ and $\sum_c\hat{p}_c=1$. Thus $\hat{\bm{p}}\in\Delta^K$. Since $S>K$, $u_e=K/S\in(0,1)$ for finite logits. Its limiting value $1$ is approached when all evidence values $e_c$ tend to zero. Finally, $S\ge\alpha_c$ for every class and $\psi(\cdot)$ is monotone on positive inputs. Therefore $\psi(S+1)-\psi(\alpha_c+1)\ge0$. Each summand in Eq.~\ref{eq:u_a} is non negative. Hence $u_a\ge0$.

\subsection{2:4 Feasibility Preservation}
\label{app:24_feasibility}
For each eligible row $i$ and complete valid block $k$, Eq.~\ref{eq:mask} selects the two local indices in $I_{i,k}^{(l)}$ and assigns mask value $1$ only to those entries. The remaining two entries receive mask value $0$. Auxiliary padding entries may be used for bookkeeping. They have score $-\infty$, remain non trainable, and cannot consume active slots. Hence every complete valid 4-block contains exactly two active trainable entries. Coordinates outside complete valid blocks are excluded from the exact 2:4 claim and are reported through the valid block fraction. They are outside the numerator and denominator of the structured sparsity guarantee. The score projection objective decomposes over independent 4-blocks. Selecting the two largest scores in each block solves the projection exactly.

\subsection{Masked Gradient Isolation}
For each entry, $W_{\text{eff},ij}=W_{ij}M_{ij}$ with the mask fixed during the mini batch. By the chain rule,
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\partial L}{\partial W_{ij}}
    =
    \frac{\partial L}{\partial W_{\text{eff},ij}}
    \frac{\partial W_{\text{eff},ij}}{\partial W_{ij}}
    =
    \frac{\partial L}{\partial W_{\text{eff},ij}}M_{ij}.
\end{equation}
Stacking this entry wise identity over the tensor gives Eq.~\ref{eq:masked_gradient}. If $M_{ij}=0$, the instantaneous task gradient with respect to $W_{ij}$ is zero, which motivates the separate structural proxy pass used by the pruning/regrowth update.

\subsection{Virtual Effective Weight Probe for Dormant Saliency}
\label{app:virtual_probe_note}
Regrowth uses a dormant link saliency distinct from the ordinary parameter gradient $\partial L/\partial W_{ij}$ under a fixed mask. The structural refresh introduces an auxiliary dense effective tensor $\widetilde{\bm{W}}_{\text{eff}}$ initialized at $\bm{W}\odot\bm{M}$ and computes proxy gradients with respect to that tensor. For a dormant coordinate, $\widetilde{W}_{\text{eff},ij}=0$, yet $\partial L_{\text{grow}}/\partial \widetilde{W}_{\text{eff},ij}$ can still be nonzero. The coordinate is treated as a virtual effective input to the proxy computation. These gradients are discarded after ranking and are never applied as ordinary updates to dormant parameters. Masked gradient isolation for training and virtual probe saliency for topology scoring are compatible.

\subsection{Signed First Order Pruning Criterion}
\label{app:signed_pruning}
Let the mask be fixed during the structural proxy pass and consider an active effective coordinate $w_{\text{eff},ij}$. Define the removal perturbation $\delta_{ij}=-w_{\text{eff},ij}$. Taylor's theorem gives
\begin{equation}
    R_{\mathcal B}(w_{\text{eff},ij}+\delta_{ij})-R_{\mathcal B}(w_{\text{eff},ij})
    =
    \delta_{ij}\frac{\partial R_{\mathcal B}}{\partial w_{\text{eff},ij}}
    +
    \mathcal{R}_{ij}.
\end{equation}
If the one dimensional second derivative along this coordinate is bounded by $L_{ij}$ on the segment between $w_{\text{eff},ij}$ and $0$, then
\[
    |\mathcal{R}_{ij}|
    \le
    \frac{L_{ij}}{2}|w_{\text{eff},ij}|^2.
\]
This gives
\[
    R_{\mathcal B}(w_{\text{eff},ij}=0)-R_{\mathcal B}(w_{\text{eff},ij})
    =
    -w_{\text{eff},ij}\frac{\partial R_{\mathcal B}}{\partial w_{\text{eff},ij}}
    +
    \mathcal{R}_{ij}.
\]
For active links, $w_{\text{eff},ij}=w_{ij}$. The leading term is negative when
\begin{equation}\label{eq:proof_signed_positive}
    w_{\text{eff},ij}\frac{\partial R_{\mathcal B}}{\partial w_{\text{eff},ij}}>0.
\end{equation}
If the leading term dominates the curvature remainder, removal decreases the proxy risk ratio. Conversely, when
\begin{equation}\label{eq:proof_signed_negative}
    w_{\text{eff},ij}\frac{\partial R_{\mathcal B}}{\partial w_{\text{eff},ij}}<0,
\end{equation}
the leading term is positive and removal increases the risk ratio to first order. Otherwise, the sign remains a local saliency ranking rather than a deterministic descent certificate. The positive part operator in Eq.~\ref{eq:C_ij} keeps only the first case as a pruning candidate.

\subsection{Local Regrowth Saliency}
\label{app:regrowth_saliency}
Let $Q(\widetilde{\bm{W}}_{\text{eff}})=L_{\text{grow}}(\widetilde{\bm{W}}_{\text{eff}})$ denote the structural probe objective evaluated at the current masked effective tensor. For a scalar virtual perturbation $\delta_{ij}$ at connection $(i,j)$,
\begin{equation}
    Q(\widetilde{w}_{\text{eff},ij}+\delta_{ij})-Q(\widetilde{w}_{\text{eff},ij})
    =
    \delta_{ij}\frac{\partial Q}{\partial \widetilde{w}_{\text{eff},ij}}
    +
    \mathcal{R}^{Q}_{ij}.
\end{equation}
If the coordinate wise curvature is bounded by $H_{ij}$ for $|\delta_{ij}|\le\eta$, then
\[
    |\mathcal{R}^{Q}_{ij}|
    \le
    \frac{H_{ij}}{2}\eta^2.
\]
Thus, to leading order, the largest achievable absolute perturbation under $|\delta_{ij}|\le\eta$ is
\[
    \eta\left|
    \frac{\partial Q}{\partial \widetilde{w}_{\text{eff},ij}}
    \right|.
\]
Ranking dormant candidates by Eq.~\ref{eq:G_ij} selects links with high local leading order influence on the KL uniform structural saliency objective. The sign is discarded. The score serves only candidate topology exploration before the valid block 2:4 projection.

\subsection{Bounded Morphological State}
The final operation in Eq.~\ref{eq:score_update} applies an element wise clamp to the interval $[-V_{\max},V_{\max}]$. Hence every updated score lies in that interval regardless of the magnitude of the unclamped momentum step. The mask is then recomputed from the bounded score tensor using Eq.~\ref{eq:mask}. Appendix~\ref{app:24_feasibility} applies at the next refresh.

\subsection{Controlled Imbalance Modulation}
Because $\operatorname{sg}[\hat{p}_{y_n}^{(n)}]$ is treated as constant during backpropagation, $\nabla_{\bm{e}}\mathcal{F}_n(t)=0$. Thus the sample level gradient is proportional to
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{F}_n(t)
    \nabla_{\bm{e}}\!\left(
    \bar{\omega}_{y_n}L_{\text{CE}}^{(n)}
    +\lambda_{\text{KL}}\mu(t)
    L_{\text{KL}}^{(n)}
    \right).
\end{equation}
Moreover, $\mathcal{F}_n(t)\ge0$, $\mu(t)\in[0,1]$, and $\bar{\omega}_{y_n}\le\omega_{\max}$ by Eq.~\ref{eq:sample_weight}. In the reported symmetric KL setting, the KL term has no additional class dependent multiplier. In the asymmetric diagnostic variant, the multiplier is capped at $\Lambda_{\max}$ by:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:asym_multiplier}
    \Lambda_{\text{asym}}^{(n)}
    =
    \min(\bar{\omega}_{y_n},\Lambda_{\max}),
    \qquad \Lambda_{\max}=10.0 .
\end{equation}
Hence focal modulation changes sample emphasis without adding an uncontrolled confidence derivative path. The supervised class weight is explicitly capped.

\subsection{Quality Gated Failure Detection Protocol}
A quality-control filter based on the entropy-derived ambiguity proxy $u_a$ remains a future evaluation. Correlation with artifacts such as glare, motion blur, and obscuration, together with accepted-versus-deferred risk at fixed coverage, would test whether the proxy has selective-prediction utility.

For controlled long tailed recognition, CIFAR-100-LT should be used to separate general imbalance behavior from the ISIC screening setting. Hardware profiling should distinguish structural 2:4 feasibility from realized acceleration. Actual speedup requires sparse Tensor Core compatible kernels and layouts such as cuSPARSELt or TensorRT sparse execution. Quality gated failure detection based on $u_a$ or related ambiguity scores is left as future work. It should be evaluated as selective prediction rather than as a clinical deployment claim.
